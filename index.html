What is FSY?

“Rise up and become a person you’re designed to be” –Dieter F. Uchtdorf.

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints prepared an event that changed a youth’s life. FSY or For the Strength of Youth is a 5-day, overnight event that happens once every 20 years. The event took place last July 2-7 2018 at Crown Legacy Hotel, Baguio City. Youths from Agoo, Baguio, San Fernando and Bauang ages 14-18 were the lucky once to experience a life changing event. This event mainly focuses on the youth’s spiritual needs, on how youth’s can be more like Christ and on how “Come unto Christ”. Classes, Musical Devotionals, Games, Talent Show and more activities are waiting for these Future Missionaries.

MEETING THE COUNSELOR AND COMPANY GROUP

July 2 marks the day when youths have to leave their phones, tablets and other gadgets on their respective houses to focus and prepare for FSY. Right after you exit the bus, the warm smiling faces of the counselors greets you as they dance with the music. Backpacks, Handbooks and Pens were distributed as the youths gather ‘round the function hall. While waiting for others to arrive, the counselors prepared mini games for the youths to not get bored on the first day. Orientations of the whole event and the Big Five rules were discussed thoroughly making sure that everyone understands. The youths then met their Counselor and Company group that will serve as their team mates throughout the whole week. Then it was followed by the assigning of rooms.

CLASSES & SOCIAL DANCE

The day started with a Morning Devotional wherein the participants meet up with their counselors to start the day with gospel study. It was then followed by a short message from the session-directing couple and classes from different couple missionaries were held. At the strike of 6:00 pm, the counselors then gave dance etiquettes to be followed for the social dance. After that the youths then prepared for the highlight of the night... the Social Night wherein youths are acknowledge to socialize with others through dancing. This is an opportunity for the youths to meet someone new and to get out of their comfort zones.

GAMES NIGHT AND FAVORITE FOOD NIGHT

Since the youths wanted to become missonaries of Christ, the counselors are now training them on how to become one. They gave lessons, manners that needs to be followed and how to introduce the gospel to them. This helps them to help people who are in need both physically and most importantly, spiritually. The highlight of the week finally came that everyone was looking forward to... The Games Night and Cheer-Offs!!. Different companies came up with their names, practiced their yell and made their banners to showcase their own companies. This is where youths showed their team work and cooperation with one another to prove that who’s team is the best of all. After all those exhausting games, Favorite Food Night came next that surely satisfied the tummies of the youths.

VARIETY SHOW & MUSICAL DEVOTIONAL

Just like what the youths did in the past days they started another day with a devotional and a gospel study. Then Variety show came next, youths were invited to showcase their unique talents to others because talents are meant to be shared not to be kept. Some sang, played guitar and piano, they danced and so much more.

The Musical Devotional is a solemn program where it focuses on the life of Jesus. It helped the youths to learn more about Jesus Christ and on howhe fulfilled his mission here on Earth. After that, the Youths then testified on what they know is true, on what they believe in.

RETRO NIGHT & FAREWELLS

The Final day. Just like the usual, they started another day with a devotional and a gospel study followed by the last message by Session-directing couple then the For the Strength of Youth activity. This program focused on the right standards of Youths, on what they should consider regarding their appearance, behaviors, dating, education and so on. Retro Night!, The last Social Dance. The youths showed their sense of fashion by wearing different retro attires and dancing through classic musics from the 80’s. The Youths then said their goodbyes to the people that they’ve met hoping to see each other again.
